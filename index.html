About Me

I am a third-year electrical engineering student at Notre Dame with a passion for innovation.

Currently, I am pursuing this passion through my startup, Mound Power, LLC. Mound Power began as a high school project intended to help baseball pitchers reduce injuries through an additional focus on lower body engagement. After our first prototype started gaining traction in the baseball community, the team dove in to create an affordable device that accurately measures the forces generated by a pitcher's lower body in real-time. Two years and seven iterations later, Mound Power is excited to have helped over 1000 pitchers train safely, have generated revenue from early adopters, and have been an exhibitor at the 2022 American Baseball Coaches Association Convention.

Today, programs across the nation, from the MLB to Little League, use Mound Power to keep their pitchers safe and at the top of their game. While the team is excited by our traction in baseball, we are aware of numerous other markets eager to get their hands on our technology and look forward to expanding into those markets in the future.

Beyond entrepreneurship, I am a member of both the Sorin Scholars and the Grand Challenge Scholars. I am currently in my fourth semester of undergraduate research with Dr. Snider's group where I have designed and built circuits and programming interfaces to test experimental adiabatic logic for improving computational efficiency.
